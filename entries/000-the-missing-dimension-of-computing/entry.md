> Why computers understand data but not value — and what it would take to change that.

---

## Abstract

For nearly seventy years, computer science has gotten remarkably good at one thing: moving data around. Yet for all of that scaffolding, a modern computer still has no idea what value is. To a machine, a transaction is just a number changing in a database. An environmental cost is a column nobody reads. A human life-and-death priority is a variable nobody thought to define.

This entry introduces **Value Engineering** as a new, foundational branch of computer science — the study of how machines can represent, preserve, transform, and reason about value. It closes by introducing **Daturrency**, a first working theory for turning passive digital money into something that actually understands what it is for.
